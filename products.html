BUILDINGS WITH MEZZANINES

The term standard Mezzanine system refers to a combination of GI steel deck, some primary built-up brand, mezzanine joists, and some support columns, too. Usually, the built-up beams tend to span down in a lateral direction. On the contrary, the Mezzanine joists span longitudinally.
This material is affordable and one of the most time-efficient ways to add extra space. It is also highly time-saving, as you can get some fantastic designs that are ready to be installed.

MULTI - STOREYED BUILDING

You will notice that pre-engineered steel building technology is widely used across most Western countries. Two factors have contributed to this popularity: the latest technology and time efficiency. Even in India, the concept of multi-storeyed construction technology has become exceptionally popular over the last decade.
Another popular advantage associated with this technology is that it is completely hassle-free, and you can rest assured of the quality. There is also an essential expansion that adds to the flexibility.
The technology is also highly effective, as it is completely resistant to any kind of earthquake. It is quite lightweight, as well, which protects its durability.

SANDWICH PU / PIR PANELS

Usually, for humongous structures like refrigerated warehouses and exhibition grounds, the use of sandwich PU/ PIR Panels is widely visible. Most people prefer the metal-faced polyurethane sandwich panels that are highly durable as well. Some specially molded tongue and groove joints can help you achieve the best fit. These panels are highly affordable and can help you with simple assembly access as well.

ROCKWOOL FIREPROOF PANELS

One of our most premium products from the list is Rockwool Fireproof Panels. What makes them unique is the use of galvanized pre-painted steel, which adds to their efficacy. The density of these panels is around 100kg/m3. These panels are resistant to both fire and sound, which adds to their efficiency. Usually, these are highly effective across cold storage facilities as well as industrial workshops and contribute to the perfect PEB.

This type of Structural Concept is generally used to build:

  • Speedy and time savvy construction than RCC.
  • Smooth and hassle - free execution at site.
  • Earthquake resistant design.
  • Greater ease of expansion and modification in steel buildings.
  • Larger spans without intermediate columns can be effectively achieved in comparison to RCC.
